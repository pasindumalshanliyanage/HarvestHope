🏥 Hunger Weakens the Body: The consequences for Health

Hunger is more than just not eating. It's a domino effect that impacts all facets of life, particularly education, health, and the vibrancy of our communities. The four main areas where hunger has a long-lasting effect are listed below.

Hunger's effects on health include:

  • Stunting and malnourishment in children, particularly those younger than five
  • Weakened immune systems, increasing vulnerability to infections
  • Vitamin deficiencies and anaemia, especially in women and pregnant mothers
  • Higher maternal and infant mortality in hunger-affected regions
Did you know? Almost 45% of deaths

in children under five worldwide are caused by malnutrition.

⚙ Hunger Hurts the Economy

Hunger prevents people from reaching their full potential. A malnourished population results in lower productivity, higher health care costs, and long-term economic stagnation.
